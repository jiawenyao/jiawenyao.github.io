tenretnI >>Internet Yami Ichi booth

2019

My booth sells four items in the Internet Yami Ichi at Chronus Art Center (CAC), Shanghai. They are Earbo, 404 bracelets, a real life Follower and some Dopamine data.

> Earbo

Earbo is a decentralized non-censorship wearable microblogging and social networking service on which users post messages through bluetooth. You Are What You Say.

> 404 Bracelets

Numbers on the 404 bracelets are invalid proxy IP addresses, which once carried their users through the Great Firewall in the mainland Chinese context.

The uniqueness of a certain IP address also makes that bracelet unique. And, when all the IPv4 addresses are used up in the near future, these bracelets will also become a testimony of the good old days.

> A Follower

Buy a real life Follower with a little bit of your privacy — your phone number and your home address —then I will follow you IRL someday.

> Dopamine Data

Three anonymous Tinder users would like to sell their tinder usage data claimed from Tinder.

The data includes daily counts of app opening, left swiping and right swiping, matches, the amount of message sent, and received.

I made a glowing pink vending interface for them, customers can pay and get a receipt-like printed data sheet.

my booth is also collected in the book A DECADE TO DOWNLOAD, which is a part of Whitney Museum Library Special Collection.
