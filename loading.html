2022

Delivery service is being advertised as seamless, as the vans that magically bring online orders, which exist primarily as a state of desire before arriving, only hours after purchase to doorsteps. The vast network of infrastructures, technologies, physical spaces, workers, and violence that makes the circulation of goods possible remains tucked out of sight for us who engage with logistics only as consumers. The existence of the logistics is similar to the rotating loading icon on a web browser, as users, this process is defined as too laborious for us to figure out what's going on inside the optical fibers and on highways.

This analogy can be extended further. The first internet protocol was developed through the physical act of driving on the open road (Ant Farm media van). The vans were traveling between nodes(truckstop network) and becoming packets just as packets shuttle through fiber optics. There are over 5 million packets being delivered daily in Germany, however, these vans, drivers and packets are in the opposite of an open-world.

This project tries to present the detours, the human & machine labor, and the undesired state of existence of a tediously verbose, 24-hour operating process as visible as the arriving of a package, through turning the packets and vans again as the media, the process the data/content, the motion the destination.
